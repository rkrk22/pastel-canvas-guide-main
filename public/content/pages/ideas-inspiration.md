Now I'm going to share my approach to finding ideas. For me, it's a **never-ending process**. As a result, I have many folders and pins on Pinterest, saved selections on other sites, **tones of screenshots and sketches**. And when I get to work, I usually already have a **large volume of prep work in progress.**

## Tools for working with references

- [**PureRef**](https://www.pureref.com/)
- [**miro.com**](http://miro.com/)
- [**fabrie.com**](https://www.fabrie.com/)

What app to use for references is not so important, proceed from what is more useful for a specific case. It is also a good idea to create mood board and references at once in Photoshop document.

I mostly prefer to use Miro. This program provides more options for working with graphics and text, as well as allows you to organize information quickly and easily. For example, I can create a board with notes and colored labels to structure information easily and quickly find the items I need. In addition, Miro has a wide range of drawing and design tools, which makes the process of creating references more creative and interesting.
