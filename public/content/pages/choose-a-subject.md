“Make your work to be in keeping with your purpose.”
 
Leonardo DaVinci

Let's define the task first. You should answer the questions: do you want to create artwork for your portfolio, to take commissions as a freelancer? Do you draw as a hobby or are you planning to get a job in a game studio. Or maybe you are making your own game?

Depending on the task, there will be a different strategies. Take a look at different options.

### If drawing is a hobby for you

If you are developing your own game or a project for a non-existing game, there is complete freedom to choose the style. You might already have some favorite games as a reference which styles you like. Indie games can have a completely various in style. The most challenging and important thing here is to create a new style, and even more challenging is to stick to it.

### You've decided to try a new profession

You need to do some research. To make something not trivial, new and interesting, you need to have at least a rough idea of what was created before. Look at what are the popular games, the trends and start collecting references by the styles, and increase your watchfulness.

And if you've never drawn art for games before, start with this page:

[What is Game Art](/read/basic-concepts)

### You're a freelancer

If your goal is to get as many commissions as possible, it is better to choose a topic that is popular by the customers. For example, it can be art for a casual mobile game. There is less competition and a great lack of specialists in the field of animation and game user interface.

### You want to get a game studio job

If it's for a very specific studio, you can try to make a portfolio in the style of the studio's games. Many have their own specific style, study it and try to draw some element from the game.
